# About Me
<span class='anchor' id='about-me'></span>

I am Gawtam Chithra Ramesh, a graduate researcher at the **KTH Royal Institute of Technology**, pursuing a Master of Science in Systems, Controls, and Robotics. I am currently conducting my Master's thesis research at **ABB Robotics**, focusing on safe, compositional translation from natural language to control policies under Signal Temporal Logic constraints.

Prior to my work in Sweden, I earned a Dual Degree (B.Tech in Engineering Design and M.Tech in Robotics) from the **Indian Institute of Technology Madras** in 2024. I have accrued targeted research experience at **Ericsson Research**, developing robust 3D Scene Graph pipelines, and within the Robotics Perception and Learning lab at KTH, evaluating taxonomy-guided vision-language models for deformable object manipulation. My research methodology relies on empirical validation and theoretically sound approaches to address current limitations in robotic generalization, object merging, and parameter sensitivity.

My primary research interests include reinforcement learning, geometric generative modeling, and the integration of high-level semantic reasoning with low-level motion primitives.
